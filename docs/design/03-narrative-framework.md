# Narrative Framework

## The Inciting Problem

The blooms are accelerating. Your Patron faction — whichever the campaign rolls determine — is dispatching field teams to investigate. Your party are new operatives. The Patron's theory about the blooms shapes your initial missions, but every faction has a theory, and the one paying your salary may not be the one telling the truth.

## Patron Relationship

Your Patron provides missions, resources, and legitimacy. At the start of a campaign, you have no reason to doubt them. Evidence that something is wrong accumulates through play, not exposition.

The Mastermind — which may or may not be your Patron — is pursuing a Scheme. If the Mastermind IS your Patron, the campaign becomes a betrayal run: the missions you're sent on may be genuine, misdirections, or setups. You won't always know which.

### Example: Bureau Patron, Convocation Mastermind

The Bureau dispatches you as bloom investigators. The Convocation has infiltrated Bureau leadership. Early missions feel legitimate. Gradually: documents contradict briefings, orders route you away from Convocation activity, returning agents disappear. The player chooses when (and whether) to act on suspicions. Siding with the Convocation is a valid path — maybe they're right.

### Example: Cartography Patron, Stillness Mastermind

The Cartography hires you to map bloom sites. The Stillness is systematically sabotaging Engines to force the Reach off necromantic infrastructure. Your mapping data is being used to identify which Engines to target next. The evidence trail: bloom sites you mapped go dark shortly after you report; Stillness operatives show up where your reports went.

## Mastermind Discovery

Evidence that the Mastermind is operating accumulates through five channels. The player never receives a single "reveal" — they piece it together.

1. **Documentary contradictions** — Documents found in dungeons conflict with Patron briefings or official records
2. **NPC behavioral shifts** — Contacts become evasive, assignments change without explanation, colleagues are reassigned or vanish
3. **Operational anomalies** — Equipment doesn't match stated mission parameters; you're sent to locations that serve someone else's agenda
4. **Faction pattern recognition** — One faction keeps appearing at sites before or after you, or their resources show up where they shouldn't
5. **Direct confrontation** — Late-game, the player can confront Patron NPCs with accumulated evidence; responses depend on whether the Patron IS the Mastermind

### Evidence Accumulation

Evidence is tracked as a counter per faction, hidden from the player but reflected in available dialogue and quest options. Each piece of contradictory evidence increments the counter for the faction it implicates.

| Threshold | Effect |
|---|---|
| 3 evidence | Inkblood (Archivist) can identify the faction as "suspicious" in Field Notes |
| 5 evidence | Ashmouth (Broker) can confront faction contacts for confirmation or denial |
| 7 evidence | The party can accuse the faction publicly — triggers Act 3 confrontation path |
| 10 evidence | Irrefutable proof — faction's Scheme is fully exposed, final dungeon unlocks |

Evidence missed (dungeons skipped, documents not found) doesn't block progression — the Scheme advances regardless, and the player faces the final confrontation with less information. Fewer evidence = fewer options in the endgame.

## The Procedural Narrative Engine

### The Six Rolls

At campaign generation, six elements are determined (mix of random selection and LLM arrangement to ensure coherence):

| Element | What It Determines | Source Pool |
|---|---|---|
| **The Patron** | Which faction employs you — the "good guys" | Any of the 5 factions |
| **The Threat** | The primary visible antagonist | Any faction except Patron |
| **The Mastermind** | The hidden power pulling strings | Any faction except Threat (can be Patron — betrayal run) |
| **The Scheme** | What the Mastermind is actually trying to accomplish | 6 authored plans |
| **The Wild Card** | Which remaining faction offers an unexpected alliance | Any uninvolved faction |
| **The Complication** | A world-state modifier changing conditions | 6 authored complications |

### The Schemes

Authored setpieces, one selected per run:

1. **Bloom Harvest** — Accelerate a bloom to consume a city-state, harvest the result
2. **Engine Seizure** — Take control of a functioning Engine and weaponize it
3. **Cascade Failure** — Trigger failures across multiple Engines to force dependence on one faction
4. **The Resurrection** — Raise an empire-era intelligence buried in the infrastructure
5. **Manufactured Crisis** — Fabricate a false bloom emergency to justify martial authority
6. **The Vault** — Open a sealed imperial vault that was sealed for very good reasons

### The Complications

World-state modifiers, one selected per run:

1. **Bloom Siege** — A bloom is spreading into the starting city, time pressure from session one
2. **Tithe Collapse** — The bone tithe system has collapsed in a region, civil unrest, no infrastructure
3. **Open War** — Two factions are already fighting, the overworld is contested
4. **Erratic Engine** — An Engine has gone haywire, dungeon environments shift unpredictably
5. **Missing Team** — A previous Bureau team went dark, their trail is your first lead
6. **Closing Passes** — Winter is shutting down routes, the overworld map shrinks over time

### LLM Role at Generation Time

Given the six rolls, the LLM arranges authored content into a coherent campaign:
- Dungeon template selection and sequencing
- NPC casting — which named characters fill which faction roles
- Evidence and document placement across dungeons
- Faction encounter ordering and escalation pacing
- Town configuration — which factions are present, what's available

The LLM does not write prose on the fly. It curates and connects pre-authored content. A game master with a box of prepared scenes, deciding which to use and in what order.

### Replayability Math

5 patrons x 4 threats x 3-4 masterminds x 6 schemes x 3 wild cards x 6 complications = hundreds of distinct campaign configurations, each feeling authored because the pieces are.

## Three-Act Structure

Flexible but escalating:
- **Act 1: Investigation** (turns 1-15) — Learn the landscape, take missions, build your roster, first hints that something is off
- **Act 2: Revelation** (turns 15-25) — The Mastermind's identity and Scheme become apparent, alliances solidify or shatter, the Wild Card appears
- **Act 3: Confrontation** (turns 25-35) — Final dungeons, faction showdowns, consequences of every choice converge
