# Characters, Classes & Progression

## Roster System

- Recruit characters from taverns, faction contacts, and dungeon encounters
- Maintain a bench of up to 12 characters
- Bring 6 into a dungeon (3 front row, 3 back row)
- Characters not in the active party don't gain XP — choices cost
- Characters can die permanently; resurrection has escalating costs
- Some recruits are faction-exclusive, gated by reputation thresholds

## Classes

Eight archetypes, each with 2-3 branching specializations chosen at level-up milestones (level 3 and level 6). Branch choices are permanent.

### Bonewarden
**Role:** Necromantic support, summons, tithe-magic

| Branch | Focus | Available At |
|---|---|---|
| Animator | Summon and command bone constructs | Level 3 |
| Tithebinder | Buff allies and debuff enemies through bone-link magic | Level 3 |
| Remnant | Sacrifice own health/stats for powerful effects | Level 6 |

**Spell costs:** Bone fragments (carried inventory), own blood (HP), or tithe tokens earned from faction work.

### Stillblade
**Role:** Melee combatant, anti-magic capability

| Branch | Focus | Available At |
|---|---|---|
| Breaker | Engine and bloom disruption, dispel effects | Level 3 |
| Warden | Tank and protector, damage absorption, threat control | Level 3 |
| Oathless | Raw damage output, berserker abilities | Level 6 |

**Unique mechanic:** Stillblades are resistant to necromantic effects but cannot benefit from allied necromantic buffs.

### Fieldwright
**Role:** Engine-tech specialist, traps, mechanical devices

| Branch | Focus | Available At |
|---|---|---|
| Artificer | Deployable gadgets and turrets | Level 3 |
| Sapper | Traps, demolition, area denial | Level 3 |
| Restorer | Healing through Engine-tech, repair constructs | Level 6 |

**Unique mechanic:** Fieldwright abilities are stronger near functioning Engines and weaker in bloom zones.

### Inkblood
**Role:** Scholar, lore skills, knowledge-based magic

| Branch | Focus | Available At |
|---|---|---|
| Cartographer | Exploration bonuses, secret detection, map reveal | Level 3 |
| Archivist | Faction intel gathering, knowledge-based debuffs | Level 3 |
| Heretic | Forbidden bloom-magic, high power at high personal cost | Level 6 |

**Unique mechanic:** Inkblood spell costs are paid in memory — temporary stat reductions that recover at rest. Over-casting causes permanent loss.

### Marcher
**Role:** Ranger/scout, overworld and dungeon navigation

| Branch | Focus | Available At |
|---|---|---|
| Pathfinder | Travel time reduction, ambush prevention, terrain bonuses | Level 3 |
| Stalker | Single-target damage, tracking, called shots | Level 3 |
| Beastkeeper | Tame and command bloom-mutated creatures | Level 6 |

**Unique mechanic:** Marchers act first in ambush rounds. Beastkeeper branch requires Ossuary Compact reputation.

### Ashmouth
**Role:** Face/bard equivalent, morale manipulation, faction negotiation

| Branch | Focus | Available At |
|---|---|---|
| Agitator | Combat buffs, enemy morale damage, war cries | Level 3 |
| Broker | Faction reputation bonuses, better trade prices, diplomatic options | Level 3 |
| Liar | Infiltration, disguise, access to faction-locked areas regardless of reputation | Level 6 |

**Unique mechanic:** Ashmouth abilities scale with the number of faction contacts in the current town — more connections means more leverage.

### Cauterist
**Role:** Combat medic, fire-based purification

| Branch | Focus | Available At |
|---|---|---|
| Surgeon | Healing, status removal, stabilize dying allies | Level 3 |
| Scorcher | Fire damage, bloom-clearing, area attacks | Level 3 |
| Flagellant | Heal others by spending own HP, powerful emergency abilities | Level 6 |

**Unique mechanic:** Cauterist fire abilities are extra effective against bloom creatures but risk spreading fire to allies in close formation.

### Hollow
**Role:** Rogue/thief, stealth, dirty fighting

| Branch | Focus | Available At |
|---|---|---|
| Filch | Lockpicking, trap disarming, loot quality bonuses | Level 3 |
| Fader | Stealth, assassination, ambush attacks | Level 3 |
| Rumor | Intel gathering, NPC manipulation, plant false information | Level 6 |

**Unique mechanic:** Hollows have a hidden "heat" meter — aggressive actions in towns raise it, making law enforcement and Bureau agents hostile. Managed through downtime or Broker connections.

## The Strategic Bind

The campaign's six rolls create the core tension:

- **You don't know the Mastermind or full Scheme at the start.** You know your Patron and the apparent Threat. Early party composition is a bet.
- **Each faction-as-threat has resistances and vulnerabilities:**
  - Stillness threat → shuts down magic-heavy parties
  - Convocation threat → bloom mutations punish melee-focused groups
  - Cartography threat → information asymmetry, need Inkbloods and Rumors to find them
  - Bureau threat → bureaucratic obstruction, need Brokers and Liars to navigate
  - Compact threat → necromantic countermeasures, need Breakers and anti-magic tools
- **Branch choices are permanent.** Picking Scorcher over Surgeon at level 3 is irreversible. If the real threat counters fire, you're stuck.
- **The bench is your insurance.** Recruiting broadly costs time but hedges against a bad read. Investing deeply in a narrow party is powerful if you guessed right, catastrophic if you didn't.
- **Faction reputation unlocks exclusive recruits.** A Beastkeeper needs Compact contacts. A Heretic needs Convocation trust. Aligning with a faction for recruits might compromise you politically.

## Leveling

- XP from combat, exploration (new rooms mapped), and quest completion
- Level cap: 10 per run
- Level-up grants: HP increase, new ability or ability upgrade, stat point allocation
- Branch choices at levels 3 and 6
- Power curve stays tight — a level 10 party is strong but not invincible against late-game encounters

### XP Curve

The XP curve is tuned to the three-act structure (35 turns):

| Campaign Turn | Expected Party Level | Milestone |
|---|---|---|
| 1-5 | 1-2 | Learning the loop, first dungeon |
| 6-10 | 3 | **First branch choice** — the bet |
| 11-15 | 4-5 | Act 1 ends, roster filling out |
| 16-20 | 6 | **Second branch choice** — doubling down or hedging |
| 21-25 | 7-8 | Act 2, Mastermind revealed, bench matters |
| 26-30 | 9 | Act 3 begins, final preparations |
| 31-35 | 10 | Endgame — level cap hit 3-5 turns before finale |

The curve flattens deliberately after level 8. The final act's progression comes from gear, synergy mastery, and faction resources — not stat increases. Bench characters brought in late can catch up via a "field promotion" mechanic: characters below the party's average level gain 50% bonus XP until they're within 1 level.

## Death & Resurrection

Death is a core pillar, not an edge case. The economy must have teeth without being purely punitive.

### Permanent Death

Characters reduced to 0 HP in combat are **downed**. Downed characters can be stabilized by a Cauterist (Surgeon) or healing item before the combat ends. If combat ends with a downed, unstabilized character, they are **dead**.

Dead characters are removed from the active party. They can be resurrected at a town's Bone Clerk — but resurrection has escalating costs.

### Resurrection Economy

| Attempt | Gold Cost | Tithe Tokens | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | 500 | 1 | -1 to a random primary stat (permanent) |
| 2nd | 1500 | 2 | -2 to a primary stat (permanent), locked out of next branch advancement |
| 3rd | — | — | Character cannot be resurrected. Permanently dead. |

Tithe Tokens are earned through Ossuary Compact missions and bloom containment work. They are scarce — a typical run yields 6-8 total across the campaign.

### Replacement vs. Resurrection

A dead character's class and branch are not unique. You can recruit a new character of the same class from a tavern. But:
- New recruits start at level 1 (field promotion helps, but they're behind)
- Branch choices must be re-made — you can choose the same branches but lose all abilities the dead character had learned
- Faction-exclusive recruits remain gated by reputation
- Unique recruits (named NPCs with special abilities) cannot be replaced

The choice between resurrecting a weakened veteran and training a fresh recruit is a genuine strategic decision.

### Ironman Considerations

In ironman mode (Phase 3), a total party kill ends the run. The bench exists as insurance — but if the entire active party dies in a dungeon, bench characters can attempt a rescue expedition to recover equipment. They cannot recover dead characters; the bodies are in a hostile dungeon.

The game can become unwinnable in ironman mode. This is intentional. The strategic bind means bad bets have consequences. The design floor: if the player has 3+ bench characters and the campaign is before turn 25, recovery is always possible (difficult, but possible). After turn 25 with fewer than 3 bench characters, the game warns the player they're in a fragile state.

## Component Inventory

Physical spell costs are the replacement for mana bars. This makes inventory management a core system, not a convenience feature.

### Component Types

| Component | Used By | Source | Stack Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bone Fragments** | Bonewarden | Loot, Compact vendors, dungeon drops | 20 |
| **Engine Charges** | Fieldwright | Engine facilities, Fieldwright crafting | 10 |
| **Ink Vials** | Inkblood | Market purchase, dungeon lore caches | 15 |
| **Cautery Supplies** | Cauterist | Market purchase, Patron resupply | 12 |
| **Bloom Samples** | Heretic branch | Bloom sites, Convocation vendors | 5 (unstable, decay after 10 turns) |
| **Tithe Tokens** | Resurrection, Tithebinder | Compact missions, bloom containment | No stack limit (rare) |

### Inventory Rules

- Each character carries 8 inventory slots (equipment worn doesn't count)
- Components stack to their limit within one slot
- The party shares a 12-slot expedition cache (accessible between combats, not during)
- Towns have unlimited storage — strategic component management happens at the expedition/town boundary
- Running out of components mid-dungeon is a real risk. Bonewardens without bone fragments can still use blood (HP) as a fallback at 2x the HP cost. Other classes have no fallback — they're reduced to basic attacks and items.

## Class Interaction Notes

### Stillblade + Bonewarden Anti-Synergy

Stillblades cannot benefit from allied necromantic buffs. This includes Tithebinder's bone-link buffs, Animator's shared defenses, and any Bonewarden ability that targets allies. This is a deliberate restriction reflecting the Stillblade's anti-magic nature.

**Design intent:** This is a discovery moment, not a trap. Both classes are available in Phase 1. Players who pair them will notice the restriction quickly (Tithebinder buffs visibly skip the Stillblade, with a "resisted" indicator). The lesson: composition matters, and reading ability descriptions pays off.

**Compensation:** Stillblades paired with Bonewardens still benefit indirectly — Animator summons draw enemy attention away from the Stillblade, and Tithebinder debuffs on enemies help everyone. The anti-synergy is on buffs, not the full toolkit.

### The Ashmouth Question

The Ashmouth (Broker) provides: reputation bonuses, trade discounts, rumor verification, and diplomatic encounter options. In a game built on an information economy with faction reputation as a gate, not having an Ashmouth is a real disadvantage.

**Design position:** This is intentional friction, not a balance problem. Every party shape has a class that makes it better — Marchers trivialize travel, Fieldwrights trivialize Engine dungeons, Inkbloods trivialize lore puzzles. The Ashmouth's advantage is broader but shallower. Without an Ashmouth:
- Rumor verification requires spending turns investigating (more expensive but always available)
- Reputation gains are slower but not blocked — quest completion is the primary rep source, not Broker bonuses
- Diplomatic encounters default to their non-Ashmouth resolution (usually harder combat or higher resource cost)

No class should be mandatory. Every class should be missed when absent.

## Downtime

Between expeditions, characters in the active party and on the bench can perform downtime activities in town. Each character gets one downtime action per town visit.

| Activity | Effect | Available To |
|---|---|---|
| **Rest** | Recover all HP, clear temporary stat penalties | All |
| **Train** | +25% XP toward next level | All |
| **Craft** | Convert raw materials into components or consumables | Fieldwright, Bonewarden |
| **Network** | +5 reputation with a faction present in town | Ashmouth (Broker) |
| **Investigate** | Verify one rumor without Ashmouth | Inkblood, Hollow (Rumor) |
| **Lay Low** | Reduce Hollow heat meter by 30 | Hollow |
| **Tend Blooms** | Stabilize one bloom sample, preventing decay | Heretic branch |

Downtime doesn't cost campaign turns — it happens during the town visit. But each character can only do one thing, creating allocation decisions.
